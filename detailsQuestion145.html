Please tell me how to connect to mobile and The Sero TV.

Function guide How to connect with The Sero TV [SmartThings Settings ]
※ Check the version of the smartphone and SmartThings app

  • - Smartphone version: Android
    6.0 or higher, iOS 10 or higher operating system - SmartThings app version: ver 1.7.33-26 or higher
    1.Turn on The Sero and run the SmartThings app on your smartphone. If you do not have the SmartThings app, you can download it from the store.
    2. The portrait mode can be set to the SmartThings app, Basically, there are 7 clocks, 3 posters, 3 photo, and 2 sound walls. In the case of Photo, up to 15 photos stored on your smartphone can be set on the screen one by one through the SmartThings app.
    3. You can set up pictures stored on your smartphone on the screen one by one. Please refer to the additional photo settings. [SmartView Connection ] Through the SmartView function of your smartphone, you can see various contents such as stored music, video, and photos.
    1.Run Smart View Run Smart View from the top bar menu on the screen.
    2. You can easily set automatic rotation and screen ratio in SmartView's Quick Run menu (floating icon). If The Sero receives a message asking you to allow the TV to connect, select the "Allow" button with the remote control.
    ※ iOS can mirror landscape mode through Airplay
    2. The service may vary depending on the smartphone you are using. [ NFC SmartView Connection] You can connect your smartphone by simply touching The Sero.
    1.Please set NFC mode. Please check if the NFC mode of the smartphone is set to the default mode.
    2. Please tag your phone on the part where the NFC mode mark is imprinted at the bottom right of The Sero. Please try to connect after about a minute after the power is turned on. Initial NFC connection may take time depending on the environment.
    ※ NFC screen mirroring is supported only for Samsung mobile devices with Android
    8.1 (SEP
    9.0) or higher.
    ※ NFC On / Off is repeatedly executed during continuous touch (repeated attempt) during NFC connection, so please be careful.
