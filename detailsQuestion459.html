I can't get the TV voice output from the speaker of the home theater.

Expected cause It may occur if it is not recognized due to interference by external devices or if it is not a genuine cable

  • Method of action Guide to setting TV and home theater functions If you connect the TV and the home theater (sound bar) to HDMI and listen to the TV sound through the speaker, connect the TV and the home theater (sound bar) to the HDMI-ARC terminal and check the settings. TV: Select Anynet + (HDMI-CEC) on Home Theater: Select to turn on the audio return channel (ARC) Select TV menu → Aeynet + (HDMI-CEC) Aeynet+ (HDMI-CEC) → Select "On" Home Theater Menu → Select Settings Sound → Audio Return Channel → Automatic Selection Check connection with HDMI (ARC) terminal The audio return channel (ARC) must be connected to the HDMI terminal of the home theater and the HDM (ARC marked terminal) terminal of the TV to make voice in both directions. Connect to HDMI terminal of home theater Connect to HDMI (ARC) terminal of TV Check HDMI cable It is recommended to use a genuine cable when connecting a TV and an external device with an HDMI cable. If the certified HDMI cable is not used, communication errors may cause disconnection. The genuine HDMI cable is printed or imprinted on the cable with Ultral High Speed HDMI, and if there is no corresponding phrase, please replace it with a genuine HDMI cable with an uncertified cable. Check the "Ultral High Speed HDMI" print or imprint on the HDMI cable. Check after removing connection of external device It may occur due to communication abnormalities with other external devices connected to the TV. Please check after removing the connection of other external devices other than TV and home theater (sound bar).
